## Overview

Mokele-mbembe is a legendary creature of the Congo River basin, particularly the Likouala swamp region around Lake Tele in the Republic of the Congo, and adjacent areas of Cameroon and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The Lingala name is commonly translated as 'one who stops the flow of rivers', a reference to the creature's supposed habit of resting in river bends. Eyewitness descriptions reported to Western researchers describe a long-necked, long-tailed animal with a body the size of an elephant or hippopotamus, short legs, reddish-brown smooth skin, and a single long tooth or horn. Cryptozoologists from the 20th century onward have proposed that mokele-mbembe is a surviving non-avian sauropod dinosaur. The first widely cited Western account is a 1913 report by German Captain Ludwig Freiherr von Stein zu Lausnitz; subsequent expeditions include those led by Roy Mackal (1980-1981), Herman Regusters (1981), Marcellin Agnagna (1983), and William Gibbons (1985 and after). No expedition has produced verifiable physical evidence. The mainstream scientific view is that the legend reflects a combination of indigenous folklore, misidentification of known animals (notably elephants swimming with raised trunks, hippopotamuses, and possibly folk memory of black rhinoceros), and, in some Western retellings, ideologically motivated 'living dinosaur' narratives — paleontologist Donald Prothero has noted that the searches today are largely conducted by creationist ministers rather than by mainstream wildlife biologists.

## Theories & Explanations

### Scientific

**Surviving non-avian sauropod** _(Roy Mackal, William Gibbons)_

Cryptozoological hypothesis that mokele-mbembe is a relict sauropod dinosaur that has survived in the remote swamps of the Congo basin.

**Folk memory or misidentification of known animals** _(BBC 2001 documentary team, Donald Prothero)_

The legend reflects folk memory of (or contemporary encounters with) known fauna such as elephants swimming with their trunks raised, hippopotamuses, or formerly resident black rhinoceros populations.

### Skeptical

**Creationist-driven cryptozoology** _(Donald Prothero)_

Most modern mokele-mbembe expeditions are organised by Christian creationist ministries seeking 'living dinosaur' evidence to challenge evolutionary biology, rather than by mainstream zoology.
